HTML Standards & Accessibility

Semantic markup, accessibility best practices, and form validation standards.

Always use semantic HTML5 elements. They improve accessibility, SEO, and code maintainability.

Good: Semantic Structure 
<header>
  <nav aria-label="Main navigation">
    <ul>
      <li><a href="/">Home</a></li>
      <li><a href="/about">About</a></li>
    </ul>
  </nav>
</header>

<main>
  <article>
    <header>
      <h1>Article Title</h1>
      <time datetime="2024-01-15">January 15, 2024</time>
    </header>
    <section>
      <h2>Section Heading</h2>
      <p>Content here...</p>
    </section>
  </article>
</main>

<footer>
  <p>© 2024 Company Name</p>
</footer>

Forms must have proper labels, error messages, and validation feedback.

Example: Accessible Form 
<form method="post" action="/submit" novalidate>
  <div class="form-group">
    <label for="email">
      Email Address
      <span aria-label="required">*</span>
    </label>
    <input 
      type="email" 
      id="email" 
      name="email" 
      required
      aria-required="true"
      aria-describedby="email-error"
      autocomplete="email"
    >
    <div id="email-error" class="error-message" role="alert" aria-live="polite"></div>
  </div>

  <div class="form-group">
    <label for="password">Password</label>
    <input 
      type="password" 
      id="password" 
      name="password" 
      required
      aria-describedby="password-hint password-error"
      autocomplete="current-password"
    >
    <div id="password-hint" class="hint">
      Must be at least 8 characters
    </div>
    <div id="password-error" class="error-message" role="alert"></div>
  </div>

  <button type="submit">Submit</button>
</form>

Use ARIA attributes for dynamic content, modals, tabs, and accordions.

Example: Modal with ARIA 
<button 
  type="button" 
  aria-label="Open contact form"
  aria-haspopup="dialog"
  data-modal-trigger="contact-modal"
>
  Contact Us
</button>

<div 
  id="contact-modal"
  class="modal"
  role="dialog"
  aria-modal="true"
  aria-labelledby="modal-title"
  aria-describedby="modal-description"
  hidden
>
  <div class="modal__overlay"></div>
  <div class="modal__content">
    <h2 id="modal-title">Contact Form</h2>
    <p id="modal-description">Fill out the form below</p>
    <button 
      class="modal__close" 
      aria-label="Close modal"
      data-modal-close
    >
      <span aria-hidden="true">×</span>
    </button>
    <!-- Form content -->
  </div>
</div>

Always include alt text, use modern formats, and implement lazy loading.

Example: Optimized Images 
<!-- Responsive image with modern formats -->
<picture>
  <source 
    srcset="hero-image.avif" 
    type="image/avif"
    media="(min-width: 768px)"
  >
  <source 
    srcset="hero-image.webp" 
    type="image/webp"
    media="(min-width: 768px)"
  >
  <source 
    srcset="hero-image-mobile.avif" 
    type="image/avif"
  >
  <source 
    srcset="hero-image-mobile.webp" 
    type="image/webp"
  >
  <img 
    src="hero-image.jpg" 
    alt="Descriptive alt text explaining the image content"
    loading="lazy"
    width="1200"
    height="600"
    decoding="async"
  >
</picture>

<!-- Decorative image -->
